An experienced AI practitioner introduces the fundamentals of text embeddings and their role in modern AI workflows. The session begins with an overview of the course structure and addresses the current AI hype cycle. Key components such as data, model operations, and cost optimization are defined. The lecture then dives into the theory of vector spaces and illustrates semantic relationships using low- and high-dimensional examples. Traditional text representations—including bag-of-words and TF-IDF—are covered before moving on to neural embeddings with Word2Vec’s CBOW and skip-gram. Transformer-based methods are explained next, highlighting positional encoding, self-attention, and BERT’s contextual embeddings. Finally, a live coding demonstration shows how to implement and compare count vectorizer, TF-IDF, Word2Vec, and BERT-based sentence embeddings, including clustering and similarity computations.
